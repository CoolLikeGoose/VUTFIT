Black Books

I'm a quitter. I come from a long line of quitters. It's amazing I'm here at all.

Here at Black Books, we stock books, if you can believe it. Proper ones. With words. Not the sort of thing you skim while sipping overpriced oat-milk nonsense in a chain café. No, these books are for reading, collecting, and occasionally throwing at unwanted customers. We don't do loyalty cards, special offers, or any of that customer is always right rubbish—because let's face it, they rarely are.

If you must come in, don't touch anything, don't ask questions, and for the love of all that is good, don't ask if we have a toilet. We don't. We barely have running water.

Tax Consulting

Yes, you read that correctly. For reasons beyond comprehension, we now offer tax consulting. Why? Because apparently, people trust me—me—to handle their finances. A man who keeps his receipts in the oven. A man whose idea of "deductible expenses" includes wine, biscuits, and emotional distress. So, if you enjoy the thrill of potential bankruptcy, audits, or possibly prison, step right up! I'll scribble some numbers, shout at a form, and declare it all “somebody else's problem.” No refunds, no guarantees, and certainly no competence. Black Books Tax Consulting: because what's the worst that could happen? (No, seriously, what? I should probably know.)

Reading Lessons

20 per lesson
  • https://drive.google.com/file/d/1UdjJ-yRGkiz832OSE4WnbQGMUrPcLgVT/view?usp=drive_link

Yes, we now offer reading lessons. No, I don't know why. Apparently, some of you have reached adulthood without mastering the art of deciphering words, and somehow, that's my problem. So, if you're truly desperate—or just enjoy being insulted—come along and let me, Bernard Black, reluctantly guide you through the mystical world of literacy. Lessons include:

  • Stop Mumbling: a masterclass in not sounding like an idiot.
  • Punctuation Exists, You Know: because commas save lives.
  • If You Ask for Harry Potter, I Will Throw You Out: self-explanatory.

There will be no patience, no encouragement, and certainly no praise. But by the end, you might be able to get through a book without drooling on it. Sign up now! Or don't. I really don't care.

Oh, so you've wandered in, gazed helplessly at the shelves, and now expect me to do your thinking for you? Fantastic. Yes, I can recommend a book, but let's be clear—this isn't some cheerful, chatty bookstore where I pretend to care about your reading journey. What are you after? Something easy? Something light? Oh, how delightful—perhaps I should also pre-chew your food while I'm at it. No, you'll take a real book. Something worthwhile. Something with words longer than your attention span. And if you ask for "that thing everyone's reading" or mention some bizarre modern nonsense like Tick Tock—which I assume is either a clock or a disease—I will personally ensure you leave with War and Peace and a firm shove out the door. So, go on, ask. But be warned: if you don't like what I recommend, that's your fault, not mine.
